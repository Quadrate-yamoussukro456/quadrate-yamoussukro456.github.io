## 🛠️ Working with your design files

seex acts as a hub for your electronic design projects. You provide the path to your project folders, and the software handles the rest.

### Linking your projects
When you open seex, you see a request to add a project folder. Click the plus button to choose the folder where you save your design files. The software scans this folder for KiCad or Altium project files. It takes a few seconds to build the index.

### Viewing your designs
Click any project in the list to see a preview. You zoom in or out using your mouse wheel. You pan around the schematics by clicking and holding the left mouse button while you move the mouse. This allows you to check your work without relying on secondary tools.

### Productivity features
The tool tracks how much time you spend on each board. You can label projects as active, finished, or archived. This status system keeps your workspace tidy. You can filter your list by selecting one of these labels at the top of the screen.

## 📋 Understanding the interface

The main window consists of three sections. 

- The sidebar on the left shows your file tree. You can collapse or expand folders here.
- The center panel shows the visual preview of your design.
- The right panel shows details like the last time you edited the file, the file size, and the project status.

If the software fails to load a file, confirm that the file format matches those listed in the project settings.
